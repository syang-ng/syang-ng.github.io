About Me

I am a third-year PhD candidate in the Computer Science Department at Yale University. I am very fortunate to be advised by Prof. Fan Zhang. Previously, I obtained my Bachalor’s and Master’s degrees from Fudan University.

My research focuses on identifying real-world security issues in blockchains systems, particularly those caused by Maximal Extractable Value (MEV), and addressing them through the design of secure and incentive-compatible decentralized systems. Feel free to reach out if you are interested in my work or collaborating with me!
